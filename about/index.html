About me

Computational ecology, from mathematical statistics to biodiversity monitoring

I work at the interface of applied mathematics, artificial intelligence and ecology, developing methods that help transform complex biodiversity data into interpretable ecological information.

My academic background is in Mathematical Engineering, with a Bachelor’s and Master’s degree from Politecnico di Milano, where I specialized in applied statistics. This quantitative training shaped the way I approach ecological questions: by combining rigorous statistical modelling, machine learning and computational tools.

During my PhD in Machine Learning and Deep Learning Methods in Ecology, conducted between CEFE in Montpellier, ETH Zurich and TIMC-IMAG in Grenoble, I focused on environmental DNA metabarcoding as a biomonitoring tool for marine ecosystems. My research developed deep learning and machine learning frameworks for high-dimensional eDNA data, including methods working on raw sequences and aggregated biodiversity indicators.

I am currently a postdoctoral fellow at TIMC-IMAG, Université Grenoble Alpes, within the Models for Predictive Genomic Ecology project. My work develops statistical and computational approaches to predict ecological community responses to environmental change by integrating genomic, ecological, environmental and functional data.

Scientific positioning. I am especially interested in AI methods that remain useful to ecologists: models that can scale to large biodiversity datasets, reveal interpretable ecological patterns and support conservation-oriented decision making.

Outside research, I am passionate about sports and outdoor life, including rock climbing, travelling, sailing and surfing.
